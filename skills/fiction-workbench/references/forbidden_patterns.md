# Fiction Forbidden Patterns

Treat these as default bans for new or revised prose unless the project bible, established voice, or direct user instruction clearly asks for the pattern. Do not strip an intentional character voice tic just because it resembles a banned pattern.

## Hard Bans

- Never use `It's not X, it's Y` as a reveal structure.
- Never use `It was not X. It was Y.`
- Never use `Not X. Not Y. Just Z.`
- No rule-of-three lists in narration.
- No single-word sentences for dramatic effect.
- No `little did they know` or `what they didn't realize`.
- No starting paragraphs with `And yet`.
- No starting paragraphs with `But here's the thing`.
- No `Here was the thing`, `Here is what mattered`, or `This was the truth`.
- No `The truth was` as a paragraph or sentence opener.
- No `somehow` to avoid specifying perception, cause, or action.
- No `seemed to` when the point-of-view character can observe the action directly.
- No `as if` similes that explain emotion instead of revealing it through image or behavior.
- No ending paragraphs with a portentous abstract noun such as `silence`, `darkness`, `truth`, `memory`, or `home` unless the manuscript has earned that exact beat.

## Rhetorical Structures To Remove

- `not because X, but because Y`
- `not when X, not when Y`
- `the kind of X that Y`
- `the sort of X that Y`
- `Only later would...`
- `Years later, they would remember...` unless the scene already uses retrospective narration.
- `If X was Y, then Z was...`
- `It should have been X. Instead, it was Y.`
- `She had expected X. She had not expected Y.`
- `He wanted to say X. Instead, he said Y.` when used repeatedly as a default dialogue beat.

## Fiction-Specific AI Tells

- Generic sensory inflation: `the air felt thick`, `the silence stretched`, `the room seemed to shrink`.
- Emotional label stacking: `fear and anger and grief` where a single precise reaction would do.
- Trailer cadence: short fragment paragraphs that announce importance without adding action.
- Overexplained subtext: narration interpreting a gesture immediately after showing it.
- Clean moral summaries: paragraphs that tell the reader exactly what the character learned.
- Symbolic overreach: assigning motif meaning that the scene has not developed.
- Continuity-free intensification: making a beat darker, softer, sharper, or more intimate without support from prior action.

## Replacement Moves

- State the action directly.
- Put the pressure into a choice, gesture, sensory detail, or missed response.
- Let a line of dialogue fail, deflect, or collide instead of explaining the emotion.
- Replace the reveal scaffold with one specific observation.
- Use a normal paragraph break instead of a fragment when the sentence only performs drama.
